# Your first output

In programming, **output** is anything your program shows to the world. A message, a number, a result.

The simplest way to produce output in JavaScript is `console.log()`.

## How it works

```javascript
console.log("Hello, World!")
```

- `console.log` is a built-in command that prints something
- The parentheses `()` are where you put what you want to print
- The quotes `"..."` wrap text — in programming, text is called a **string**

## Your turn

Print exactly: `Hello, World!`

Pay attention to capitalisation and punctuation — `Hello, World!` is not the same as `hello, world!`.
